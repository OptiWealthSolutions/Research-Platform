# PRODUCT

## Register
product

## Product Purpose
Macro Research Terminal: aggregates macro/financial research papers from central
banks and institutions (BIS, ECB, Fed, BoE, IMF, NBER, RePEc/NEP, Elsevier/SSRN),
deduplicates and auto-tags them, and runs a FinBERT analysis layer (sentiment,
key terms, summary) on recent papers. The point is not to read papers, it is to
convert a flood of research into a fast read on where macro sentiment is leaning,
broken down by region/currency, so a trader can act.

## Users
Single primary user: an active retail FOREX trader who also invests across asset
classes. Sits in front of the screen during market hours. Scans fast, drills down
selectively. Cares about: directional bias (bullish/bearish) per currency bloc
(USD, EUR, GBP, JPY, CNY), what changed in the last 24h–7d, and which institution
is saying it. Reads English research, French UI prose is fine but terminal labels
stay English/mono.

## Tone
Terminal-native, dense, professional. Bloomberg-cockpit logic: information per
square inch, color-coded, keyboard-driven, zero decoration that does not carry
data. Confident, not playful. The screen should feel like an instrument, not a
content site.

## Anti-references
- Generic SaaS card grids, marketing whitespace, hero sections.
- Medium-style reading layouts (this is not a blog).
- Pastel/rounded "friendly" dashboards.
- Anything that hides data behind clicks when it could be on the glass.

## Strategic principles
- Data-first: sentiment and deltas visible without interaction.
- Scannable: dense table/feed a trader can sweep top-to-bottom in seconds.
- Decision support: aggregate the signal (net sentiment per region/source), do
  not just list documents.
- Speed: keyboard command bar, instant filtering, no modal-first flows.
